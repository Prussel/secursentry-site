Privacy Policy

Operated by Prussel Holdings Limited trading as SecurSentry  ·  Effective: March 2025

We take your privacy seriously — which makes sense, given we're building a product to help businesses take security seriously too. This policy explains what personal data we collect when you visit secursentry.com, why we collect it, how we use it, and your rights under UK GDPR and EU GDPR.

Section 01

Who We Are

The data controller for secursentry.com is:

Company Prussel Holdings Limited (trading as SecurSentry)
Website secursentry.com
Email [email protected]

We will update this policy with our registered address and company number once formally registered.

Section 02

What Personal Data We Collect

We currently operate a holding page at secursentry.com, which collects the following information when you register your interest:

Data you provide directly

  • Your name
  • Your work email address
  • Your company size (selected from a range, e.g. 1–10, 11–25)

Data collected automatically

  • IP address — collected by Cloudflare as part of normal internet traffic handling
  • Approximate location data derived from IP address
  • Browser type, device type, and operating system (via Cloudflare analytics)
  • Pages visited and time spent on site (via Cloudflare Web Analytics)

We use Cloudflare Web Analytics, which is a privacy-focused tool. It does not use cookies, does not fingerprint individual users, and does not share data with advertising networks.

Section 03

Legal Basis for Processing

Under UK GDPR and EU GDPR, we must have a lawful basis for processing your personal data.

Consent (Article 6(1)(a))

When you complete and submit the interest registration form, you are actively providing your personal data and consenting to us contacting you about the SecurSentry product launch. You may withdraw this consent at any time by emailing [email protected].

Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f))

We use Cloudflare analytics to understand how visitors use our website so we can improve it. This involves collecting anonymised, aggregated data about site usage. We consider this to be in our legitimate interests as a business, and we have assessed that it does not override your privacy rights, given the privacy-preserving nature of the tool used.

Section 04

How We Use Your Data

We use the personal data we collect for the following purposes:

  • To contact you when SecurSentry launches or reaches a relevant milestone
  • To understand the size and type of businesses on our waitlist, so we can prioritise product development accordingly
  • To respond to any questions or enquiries you send us
  • To monitor and improve the performance and usability of our website

We do not use your data for automated decision-making or profiling. We do not sell your data to third parties. We do not use your data for advertising purposes.

Section 05

Third-Party Processors

We share your data with a small number of trusted third-party processors who help us operate our website and manage submissions. All processors are bound by data processing agreements and handle your data only on our instructions.

Formspree (form submissions)

Formspree, Inc. processes the data you submit via our registration form. Formspree is based in the United States. For transfers of personal data from the UK or EU, Formspree relies on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) as the lawful transfer mechanism.

Cloudflare (hosting and analytics)

Cloudflare, Inc. hosts our website and provides analytics and security services. Cloudflare processes IP addresses and traffic data as part of its standard operations. Cloudflare participates in the EU-US Data Privacy Framework and provides SCCs for UK/EU transfers.

Cloudflare Privacy Policy →

No other processors

We do not currently use any other third-party services that process personal data, including advertising networks, social media trackers, or CRM platforms. We will update this policy if this changes.

Section 06

How Long We Keep Your Data

We retain waitlist registration data (name, email, company size) for as long as is necessary to contact you about the SecurSentry product launch, and for a reasonable period thereafter. Specifically:

  • If SecurSentry launches, we will contact you and give you the opportunity to opt in or out of further communications at that point
  • If you ask us to remove your data at any time, we will do so within 30 days
  • If the product does not launch and we decide to wind down, we will securely delete all waitlist data

Cloudflare retains analytics data in accordance with its own retention policy. Cloudflare Web Analytics data is retained for up to 30 days by default.

Section 07

International Data Transfers

Our processors Formspree and Cloudflare are based in the United States. Transfers of your personal data to these processors are made under appropriate safeguards:

  • EU personal data: Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission
  • UK personal data: International Data Transfer Agreements (IDTAs) or addendums to SCCs as approved by the UK ICO

You can request further information about these transfer mechanisms by contacting us at [email protected].

Section 08

Your Rights

Under UK GDPR and EU GDPR, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

Right of access

Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you and how we use it.

Right to rectification

Ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data we hold about you.

Right to erasure

Ask us to delete your personal data. We'll comply unless we have a legal reason to retain it.

Right to restrict

Ask us to pause processing your data in certain circumstances.

Right to portability

Receive your personal data in a structured, machine-readable format where applicable.

Right to object

Object to processing based on our legitimate interests at any time.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at [email protected]. We will respond within one month. There is no charge for making a request.

Section 09

Cookies

Our website does not use cookies for tracking, advertising, or analytics purposes. Cloudflare may set strictly necessary cookies for security and performance purposes (such as preventing DDoS attacks). These are functional cookies and do not require consent under UK or EU law.

We do not use third-party advertising cookies, social media pixels, or any other non-essential cookies.

Section 10

Children's Privacy

Our website and product are directed at businesses and business professionals. We do not knowingly collect personal data from anyone under the age of 16. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

Section 11

How to Make a Complaint

If you are unhappy with how we have handled your personal data, please contact us first at [email protected] so we can try to put it right. If you are not satisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the relevant supervisory authority:

UK residents

Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)
ico.org.uk  ·  0303 123 1113

EU residents

You may contact the data protection authority in your EU member state. A full list is available at edpb.europa.eu.

Section 12

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time — for example when we add new features, engage new processors, or when the law changes. When we make material changes, we will update the effective date at the top of this document.

We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Continued use of our website after changes have been posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
